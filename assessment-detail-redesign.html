Finding

The evidence suggests that the Strategic Case is well-structured, clearly outlining the need for HS2 and demonstrating its potential to alleviate rail congestion and promote economic growth. However, while the case contains substantial arguments for its necessity, it falls short in fully quantifying all benefits, especially regarding the transformative impacts on economic location decisions, which is expected for a GREEN rating.

Evidence
"The Strategic Case provides compelling evidence that HS2 offers the only viable long-term solution to overcrowding on the rail network transport and will be a major contributor to the objective of levelling up the economy."
FBC Section 3.2, Page 47
"The economic case does not fully quantify all the benefits set out in the strategic case such as the transformative benefits from changes in business location decisions." • "HS2 continues to generate 42 per cent journey time improvements between London and the West Midlands and 35 per cent to the North West."
FBC Section 4.1, Page 89
Recommendation
Action Required
To achieve a GREEN rating, HS2 Ltd should enhance its Strategic Case by strengthening the quantification of non-monetised benefits, particularly those tied to economic rebalancing and transformation. Additionally, further validation through independent review could substantiate claims made regarding the impact of HS2 on local economies.
